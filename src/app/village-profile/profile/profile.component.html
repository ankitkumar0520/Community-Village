Martam Village, East Sikkim

Nestled in the serene hills of East Sikkim, Martam village offers a perfect blend of traditional Sikkimese culture and modern comfort. Known for its organic farming, pristine monasteries, and breathtaking views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range, this village provides an authentic Himalayan experience.

Village Description

Martam is a picturesque village nestled in the Eastern part of Sikkim, India. Surrounded by lush forests and terraced paddy fields, it offers a serene retreat for nature enthusiasts. The village is adorned with diverse flora and fauna, making it a haven for bird watchers and botanists. Visitors can experience the rich cultural heritage of the local communities, who are known for their traditional lifestyles and warm hospitality.

Tourist Attractions

Fambong La Wildlife Sanctuary

Located nearby, this sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife species and offers trekking opportunities.

Rumtek Monastery

A significant Buddhist monastery situated close to Martam, known for its architecture and spiritual ambiance.

Trekking Trails

The region offers several trekking routes with panoramic views of the Himalayas, including the Kanchenjunga range.

Leader Profiles

Tenzing Sherpa

President

Tenzing Sherpa has been serving as the President of the Martam Village Committee for over a decade. With a background in community development, he has been instrumental in implementing sustainable tourism practices and preserving the village's cultural heritage. His leadership has led to the establishment of several eco-friendly initiatives, benefiting both the environment and the local community.

Lhamo Dolma

Secretary

Lhamo Dolma, the Secretary of the Martam Village Committee, is a dedicated educator and cultural ambassador. She has been pivotal in organizing cultural exchange programs and educational workshops for both locals and tourists. Her efforts have significantly contributed to the preservation and promotion of Martam's rich traditions.
